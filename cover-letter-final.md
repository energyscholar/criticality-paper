Dear Editors,

We submit "Convergent Discovery of Critical Phenomena Mathematics Across Disciplines" as a Perspectives article for FACETS.

This paper documents a pattern that has not been mapped in full: at least six scientific disciplines independently derived functionally corresponding mathematics for detecting critical transitions, largely without awareness of each other's work, over nine decades (1935–2025). We provide quantitative citation network evidence that cross-domain citation rates remained significantly below random-mixing expectations during the formative period (1987–2010), and classify each surveyed instance by discovery type (independent derivation, domain transfer, foundational cross-domain contribution, empirical precursor).

The convergence pattern extends into climate science: we trace a chain from Stommel's thermohaline circulation models (1961) through bifurcation analyses of the Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation to deep learning early warning signal classifiers that generalize across ecology, cardiology, and climate. Two McGill emeriti — one in cardiology, one in climate science — applied the same bifurcation structures in different physical systems and did not recognize the mathematical equivalence until connected by an outside investigator.

We believe this paper fits the Perspectives category because its contribution is a new viewpoint on established science, not novel mathematics. The cross-domain taxonomy, citation analysis, and concrete examples of institutional proximity without disciplinary awareness offer a synthesis that no single field has produced.

We suggest Kimberly Strong (physics) as subject editor whose expertise spans the physical sciences represented in this work.

We respectfully request consideration for an APC waiver. Both authors are independent researchers with no institutional affiliation, no grant funding, and no institutional support of any kind. This paper was produced entirely from personal resources. We understand that FACETS considers waiver requests on a case-by-case basis and have confirmed that no other funding sources are available to us.

Sincerely,
Bruce Stephenson and Robin Macomber
